## 2. Generational Insurance Behavior Profiles

### Gen Z (16-28 years old)

**Source**: Prudential SG60 Poll (N=1,000, July 2025), Capco 2023

| Dimension | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| Retirement confidence | 51% believe they can retire well |
| Retirement planning | **72% have NO retirement plan** |
| Target retirement age | 54% aim for 60, 20% aim for 50 |
| Income strategy | 41% seek multiple income streams |
| Investment preference | Index funds/ETFs (not insurance) |
| Insurance reliance | **Lower than all other generations** |
| Work-life balance | 60% don't prioritize WLB over career growth |
| Micro-retirement | 22% interested in multiple career breaks |
| Remote work | 32% want remote work for travel-work balance |
| Top product bought | Mobile/gadget insurance (18%) — Capco |
| Financial preparedness | 54% feel financially prepared (AIA 2025) |

**Key Insight**: Confidence-planning paradox — high optimism, minimal action.
They are **the most under-insured cohort** but **least motivated to buy**.

### Millennials / Gen Y (29-44 years old)

**Source**: Prudential SG60, Capco 2023, AIA 2025

| Dimension | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| Retirement confidence | 45% (lower than Gen Z) |
| Investment preference | Index funds/ETFs, but starting to value insurance |
| Top products bought | Travel insurance (23%), Health insurance (63%) — Capco |
| Life insurance | Starting to buy, especially after marriage/kids |
| Financial preparedness | Moderate — squeezed by housing + childcare costs |
| Key concern | Cost of living (50%), Job security (35%) |
| Insurance attitude | 68% intend to buy, only 44% feel knowledgeable |

**Key Insight**: Peak buying cohort for protection products.
Aware of need, but **knowledge gap** is major barrier.

### Gen X / Xennials (45-55 years old)

**Source**: Prudential SG60, Capco 2023

| Dimension | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| Retirement confidence | **38%** (lowest of all generations) |
| Top products bought | Life insurance (55%), Income protection (16%) — Capco |
| Financial preparedness | **34% feel prepared** (age 40-49, AIA 2025) — lowest |
| Key concern | Healthcare costs (56%), retirement adequacy |
| Insurance behavior | Review & top-up existing coverage |
| IP sensitivity | Most concerned about IP premium increases |

**Key Insight**: Sandwich generation. **Most anxious about finances** despite
having the most coverage. IP affordability is their #1 insurance concern.

### Baby Boomers (55+ years old)

**Source**: Prudential SG60, Capco 2023

| Dimension | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| Retirement regret | 94% would change financial planning if they could start over |
| Ideal start age | Wish they started at **28** (actual average: **40**) — 12 years earlier |
| Top regret | 61% didn't form financial habits early enough |
| Could have retired earlier | 49% believe so |
| Top products bought | Long-term savings (50%), Property (25%), Auto (31%), Pet (11%) — Capco |
| CareShield Life | Core concern — long-term care financing |
| IP attitude | Most reluctant to downgrade, highest loyalty |

**Key Insight**: Hindsight generation. Valuable for "what would you tell your younger self"
scenarios. Highest insurance loyalty, but most concerned about **LTC costs**.

## 6. Protection Gap by Income (LIA PGS 2022)

### Household Income Context

| Type | Ratio to Average |
|------|-----------------|
| Single-income household | **79%** of average |
| Dual-income household | **127%** of average |

### Gap Pattern by Income (Estimated from PGS)

| Income Level | Mortality Gap | CI Gap | Key Behavior |
|-------------|--------------|--------|-------------|
| Low (bottom 20%) | ~35-40% | ~85-90% | Rely heavily on CPF, minimal private insurance |
| Lower-middle | ~25-30% | ~80% | Basic term life, minimal CI |
| Middle | ~20% | ~75% | Term + some whole life, basic IP |
| Upper-middle | ~15% | ~65% | Comprehensive coverage, IP Class B1+ |
| High (top 20%) | ~10% | ~50% | Multiple policies, Class A IP, wealth planning |

### Housing Type as Wealth Proxy

| Housing | Approx. Income Percentile | Insurance Behavior |
|---------|--------------------------|-------------------|
| HDB 1-2 room | Bottom 10-15% | Minimal, depend on government schemes |
| HDB 3 room | 15-35% | Basic coverage, MediShield Life only |
| HDB 4 room | 35-60% | Growing coverage, may have basic IP |
| HDB 5 room / Exec | 60-80% | Decent coverage, IP + some CI |
| Condo | 80-95% | Comprehensive, Class B1/A IP |
| Landed | Top 5% | Premium coverage, estate planning |

| Segment | Population | Key Insurance Relevance |
|---------|-----------|------------------------|
| Singapore Citizens | ~3.6M | Full CPF access, MediShield Life, DPS |
